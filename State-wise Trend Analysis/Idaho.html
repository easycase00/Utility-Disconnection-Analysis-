Key Findings for Idaho Utility Disconnections

 

Although the state of Idaho has other electric companies, the data only covered the City of Idaho Falls Electric Company. The City of Idaho Falls Electric Company is a municipal utility and serves one-thirds of the city of Idaho Falls.

 

 

·         Pre-Covid (2017-2019)
o   In January 2017, the state had 3 disconnections. Five months into the year the state reached its peak in disconnections at 849. By the end of the year the state hit its lowest at just one disconnection.
o   The peak of 2018 was in October with 1,646, with the year starting with 48 disconnections and ending with 2 disconnections.
o   The year of 2019 started with no disconnections, by April the state had reached its peak at 1,507 disconnections and by December it just had 4 disconnections.
o   In conclusion, the disconnections in the pre-Covid years for the state of Idaho remained consistent.
·         Covid (2020)
o   In January there were 7 disconnections in the state of Idaho.
o   The year’s peak was reached in October with 1,066 disconnections.
o   By the end of 2020, disconnections had gone back down to 3.
o   In conclusion, in the state of Idaho, the present of Covid did not impact utility disconnections no more than normal. In fact, 201 and 2019 peaks were higher than 2020.
·         Post-Covid (2021-2023)
o   In January 2021, the state had 5 disconnections.  Six months into the year the state reached its peak in disconnections at 810. The year ended with a higher number of disconnections (124) than at the start of the year.
o   The peak of 2022 was in March with 527 disconnections, with the year starting with 2 disconnections and ending with in April with 82 disconnections.
o   There was no data for 2023.
o   In conclusion, the disconnections in the post-Covid years for the state of Idaho remained consistent.
